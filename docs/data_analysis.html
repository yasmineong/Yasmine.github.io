There has been an increase in second-hand car prices till around 2013 (though this may vary, eg. Bangladore and Delhi experienced increases earlier) but seems to be mostly coming down after that, leading to a growing difference in prices of first and second hand cars. If we were to do some predictions (predictive pricing models will not be covered here), the decreasing trend can provide a benchmark against which sellersâ€™ asking prices can be compared. If sellers consistently ask for prices significantly higher than the predicted values for similar cars, it may indicate that they are attempting to exploit buyersâ€™ lack of information or conceal defects in the vehicles. This widening price gap could result from a worsening lemon market problem, where buyers become increasingly wary of purchasing used cars due to concerns about hidden defects or poor quality. Therefore, there is also a need to investigate the cause of this growing price difference.
